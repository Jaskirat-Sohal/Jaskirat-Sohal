## What I actually do
 
I sit at the intersection of biology and machine learning, which mostly means I spend my days convincing neural networks that biological data is *supposed* to look messy.
 
- **Multi-omics ML for colorectal cancer** — integrated gut microbiome and metabolome data (347 paired samples) with Random Forest, XGBoost, and MOFA+ to classify CRC stages, ran SHAP analysis to pull out biologically meaningful microbial and metabolite signatures, then stress-tested it on an independent European cohort. That last part broke for a while — two labs, two taxonomy systems, zero overlap in feature names — until a token-matching fix and a batch-correction method got the datasets talking to each other
- **Medical image segmentation** — trained U-Net architectures at IISc Bangalore to draw boundaries doctors would actually trust
- **Meta-analysis** — pulled apart glioblastoma literature to find out if cutting out more tumor really means living longer (spoiler: it's complicated)
- **Public health data science** — ran the numbers on COVID-19 and PCOS/PCOD onset in young women, then got it published
- **Molecular docking** — mutated a single residue (His-571) on an enzyme and simulated whether it still did its job
