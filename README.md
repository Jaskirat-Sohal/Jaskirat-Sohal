## What I actually do
 
I sit at the intersection of biology and machine learning, which mostly means I spend my days convincing neural networks that biological data is *supposed* to look messy.
 
- **Multi-omics ML for colorectal cancer** — combined gut microbiome and metabolite data from 347 patients to build models that can classify colorectal cancer stages, then tested them against a completely different dataset from another country to see if the findings actually held up. They didn't at first — the two datasets didn't even name things the same way — so I had to build a fix before the validation would work at all
- **Medical image segmentation** — trained U-Net architectures at IISc Bangalore, working up through increasingly gnarly datasets: mitochondria in cell microscopy images first, then blood vessels in retinal scans, then tumors in breast cancer imaging. Each one taught the model a new definition of "boundary"
- **Meta-analysis** — pulled apart glioblastoma literature to find out if cutting out more tumor really means living longer (spoiler: it's complicated)
- **Public health data science** — designed and ran a cross-sectional survey on whether COVID-19 had anything to do with earlier PCOS/PCOD onset in young women, then collected the data myself, cleaned it, and ran the full EDA and statistical analysis end to end. Went from a blank survey form to a published paper
- **Molecular docking** — mutated a single residue (His-571) on an enzyme and simulated whether it still did its job
